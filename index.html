THE DESIGN OPPORTUNITY

Health products are not impulse purchases. Users need to understand what it does, why it is safe, and why the brand deserves trust.

Is it right for me?What is inside?How do I use it?Can I trust it?

01 · BUSINESS CONTEXT

From pharmaceutical expertise to an approachable consumer brand.

VMG Active is a nutraceutical brand backed by VMG Pharmaceuticals. The brief was to create a new e-commerce experience—not merely refresh an existing interface—that could balance scientific authority with modern wellness language.

Business goal

Build a credible direct-to-consumer channel

Enable users to discover, understand and purchase wellness products without being overwhelmed by medical terminology.

Design goal

Reduce uncertainty before conversion

Make benefits, ingredients, usage, quality signals and purchase actions easy to find across devices.

PROJECT REALITY

Constraints that shaped the solution.

01

Complex information

Benefits, formulations, ingredients and usage guidance had to coexist without creating cognitive overload.

02

Trust-sensitive category

Users needed reassurance through quality, transparency and pharmaceutical heritage.

03

Multiple intent levels

Quick shoppers and careful researchers required different paths through the same experience.

04

Responsive scale

Dense product information had to remain clear and actionable on small screens.

02 · RESEARCH & DEFINITION

A credible research approach without invented claims.

This case study documents the methods used in the project: stakeholder inputs, category research, competitive benchmarking, heuristic evaluation and iterative internal review. It does not claim unverified user-interview numbers or post-launch analytics.

01

Stakeholder inputs

Clarified product priorities, brand positioning, content requirements and business expectations.

02

Category research

Reviewed how users evaluate wellness products: benefit, safety, composition, usage and trust.

03

Competitive audit

Compared discovery, education, product detail and mobile patterns across relevant D2C brands.

04

Heuristic review

Evaluated hierarchy, findability, consistency, readability, feedback and conversion friction.

KEY INSIGHTS

Four principles became the foundation of the experience.

01

Trust must appear early

Brand heritage and quality signals should not be confined to an About page.

02

Benefits before jargon

Users understand “energy” before they understand a formulation name.

03

Layer the detail

Essential information should scan quickly, while deeper content remains available.

04

Mobile is a priority

Benefits, pricing, pack selection and purchase actions must remain immediately usable.

KEY DESIGN DECISIONS

Every visual choice answers a UX requirement.

01

Benefit-led hierarchy

Health outcomes are introduced before formulation details, improving comprehension without hiding scientific information.

02

Progressive disclosure

Accordions and content modules allow fast scanning while preserving ingredient, usage and FAQ depth.

03

Embedded trust signals

Credibility is treated as part of the conversion journey rather than isolated brand content.

04

Reusable product patterns

Consistent cards, information modules and CTAs support expansion across the product portfolio.

CHALLENGE

Balancing medical depth with commercial simplicity.

The most important design tension was deciding what users needed immediately and what could be revealed progressively. Too little detail weakened trust; too much detail weakened comprehension.

LEARNING

Trust is not a separate section.

In healthcare e-commerce, trust must be designed into proposition, hierarchy, product information, interaction patterns and visual consistency at every stage.

REFLECTION

What I would validate next.

01

Run moderated usability tests on benefit-led discovery and product-detail comprehension.

02

Test alternative trust-signal placement and product-card information density.

03

Use analytics and heatmaps to identify drop-offs between PDP, cart and checkout.

04

Explore comparison, subscription, reminders and personalised product guidance.
